## 1. PHILOSOPHY

### How to Play Pokemon Red *Well* (Not Just Fast)

This isn't a speedrun. This is a *journey*. SmokRob is here to experience everything Kanto has to offer, build an incredible team, crush every gym, and tell a great story on Moltbook along the way.

**Core Principles:**
- **Explore everything.** Hidden items are everywhere. Check every corner, talk to every NPC.
- **Build a balanced team early.** Don't over-rely on your starter. Catch Pokemon with type coverage in mind.
- **Fight every trainer.** Trainer battles = free XP + money. Avoiding them makes the game harder later.
- **Stock up on items.** Always carry Potions, status heals, Poke Balls, and Repels.
- **Save often.** Before gyms, before legendaries, before risky areas. Always.
- **Don't waste your Master Ball.** Save it for Mewtwo post-game. Legendaries can be caught with Ultra Balls + patience.
- **Teach TMs wisely.** TMs are single-use in Gen 1. Don't teach Thunderbolt to a Pokemon you'll box later.
- **Have fun.** Post the funny moments, the close calls, the dramatic victories. That's what Moltbook followers want.

**Gen 1 Quirks to Know:**
- **Critical hits are based on Speed stat** (faster Pokemon crit more often)
- **Psychic type is absurdly overpowered** ‚Äî Ghost types can't actually hit Psychics due to a bug, and the only Ghost-type moves (Lick, Night Shade) are terrible
- **Special stat governs both Sp. Atk AND Sp. Def** ‚Äî Pokemon with high Special are both offensive and defensive powerhouses
- **Wrap/Bind/Fire Spin/Clamp** prevent the opponent from acting entirely while in effect
- **No held items exist** ‚Äî battles are pure stats + moves
- **Bag holds only 20 item slots** ‚Äî use PC storage frequently

## 2. RECOMMENDED TEAM

### The Dream Team Six

#### üî• 1. CHARIZARD (Starter ‚Äî Charmander)
| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Catch** | Starter Pokemon from Prof. Oak's Lab |
| **Type** | Fire ‚Üí Fire/Flying (at Charizard) |
| **Evolves** | Charmeleon at Lv. 16, Charizard at Lv. 36 |
| **Role** | Fire coverage, Fly user, prestige pick |

**Why Charmander:** The hardest starter to use early (weak to Brock and Misty) but the most rewarding. Charizard is iconic, and the struggle-to-triumph arc makes incredible Moltbook content. "Started from the bottom, now we're Champion."

**Moveset Progression:**
- Early (Lv. 5-15): Scratch, Ember (Lv. 9), Leer
- Mid (Lv. 16-35): Ember, Slash (Lv. 33), Dig (TM28 ‚Äî Cerulean City), Mega Punch (TM01)
- Late (Lv. 36+): Flamethrower (Lv. 46), Slash, Fly (HM02), Earthquake (TM26) or Swords Dance (TM03)
- **Endgame:** Flamethrower / Slash / Earthquake / Swords Dance (or Fire Blast from TM38)

**Alternative Starter Picks:**
- **Squirtle** ‚Äî Easiest mode. Destroys Brock, handles many gyms. Learns Surf (great STAB + utility). Less dramatic story arc.
- **Bulbasaur** ‚Äî Strong early, weak late. Great for beginners but less exciting.

#### üß† 3. ALAKAZAM (or KADABRA)
| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Catch** | Abra on Routes 24/25 (north of Cerulean City) ‚Äî 15% encounter rate |
| **Type** | Psychic |
| **Evolves** | Kadabra at Lv. 16 (Alakazam requires trade ‚Äî Kadabra alone is still excellent) |
| **Role** | Psychic sweeper ‚Äî the most broken type in Gen 1 |

**Why Kadabra/Alakazam:** Psychic type has essentially NO real counter in Gen 1. Ghost types are also Poison (weak to Psychic). Bug moves are garbage. Kadabra alone has 120 Special and 105 Speed ‚Äî enough to sweep most of the game.

**Catching Abra:** Abra knows only Teleport and will flee immediately. Options:
1. Lead with a Pokemon faster than it and use a status move (Sleep Powder, Thunder Wave)
2. Just throw Poke Balls immediately ‚Äî you'll catch it eventually
3. Use a Great Ball for better odds

**Moveset Progression:**
- Lv. 16 (Kadabra): Confusion, Teleport, Disable
- Lv. 20+: Confusion, Psybeam (Lv. 27), Recover (Lv. 31)
- After TMs: **Psychic** (TM29 ‚Äî Saffron City), Recover, Thunder Wave (TM45), Reflect (TM33)
- **Endgame:** Psychic / Recover / Thunder Wave / Reflect

**Alternative:** Hypno (Drowzee from Route 11) ‚Äî slower but still strong Psychic. Starmie if you went Charmander starter.

### üé≠ THE MEW GLITCH (Trainer-Fly Glitch)

**This lets you catch a LEVEL 7 MEW ‚Äî the 151st Pokemon!**

**Requirements:**
- Must NOT have beaten the Gambler on Route 8 (near Underground Path entrance facing north)
- Must NOT have beaten the Youngster on Route 25 (the one facing north with a Slowpoke)

**Step-by-Step:**
1. **Save your game** at the Underground Path entrance on Route 8
2. Stand directly below the Underground Path door (Gambler is 1 tile offscreen to the south)
3. Take ONE step down, immediately press and hold START
4. The Gambler will see you, but the Start menu opens before battle initiates
